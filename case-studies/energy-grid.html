About National Power Grid

National Power Grid (NPG) manages electricity distribution across 3,000+ substations serving 40 million households. With renewables contributing 45% of total generation, grid stability had become increasingly challenging due to solar and wind volatility.

Employees

The challenge

Renewable volatility outpacing legacy systems

With renewable energy contributing 45% of generation, NPG's legacy SCADA-based system could not keep pace with the complexity of managing distributed energy resources, battery storage, and real-time demand response. Grid operators averaged 18 minutes to detect and respond to imbalance events, incurring $210M annually in penalty costs and emergency power purchases.

Top challenges

  • 18-minute average response time to grid imbalance events
  • $210M annual penalties from frequency instability
  • 45% renewable mix causing unpredictable generation swings
  • Legacy SCADA unable to coordinate 3,000+ distributed nodes

Skills deployed

  • 24 decentralized node agents with 3 regional coordinators
  • Multi-agent reinforcement learning for load balancing
  • Real-time weather integration for renewable forecasting
  • Battery storage optimization engine
The solution

Decentralized agents balancing the grid in real-time

FRACIFY deployed 24 node-level agents and 3 regional coordinators. Each node agent manages local load balancing while communicating with neighbors for global grid stability. The agents predict renewable generation with 99.3% accuracy at 4-hour horizons and automatically dispatch battery storage, demand response, and grid interchanges to maintain frequency stability.
