The challenge

The National Power Grid (NPG) manages electricity distribution across 3,000+ substations serving 40 million households. With renewable energy sources now contributing 45% of total generation, grid stability had become increasingly challenging. Solar and wind volatility was causing frequency fluctuations that required rapid load balancing decisions.

NPG's legacy SCADA-based system could not keep pace with the complexity of managing distributed energy resources, battery storage, and real-time demand response. Grid operators were averaging 18 minutes to detect and respond to imbalance events, leading to $210M annually in penalty costs and emergency power purchases.

The solution

FRACIFY deployed a decentralized agent swarm with 24 node-level agents and 3 regional coordinators. Each node agent manages local load balancing while coordinating with neighbors for global grid stability.

Technology stack

FRACIFY Orchestrator Multi-agent RL SCADA integration Weather forecasting API Battery optimization engine Real-time frequency monitoring

Results

  • 99.3% prediction accuracy for renewable generation forecasting at 4-hour horizon
  • 23% reduction in energy waste through improved load balancing
  • 18 minutes to 45 seconds — 96% faster imbalance detection and response
  • 3.2 million tons CO2 emissions avoided annually
  • $62M annual savings from reduced penalties and optimized power purchasing
