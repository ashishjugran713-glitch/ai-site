The Problem

Imperonation is the #1 hiring fraud vector

Here's the root cause of imperonation: you have a different tool for AI interviews, a different one for coding assessments, another for video interviews, and your hiring flow lives in a separate ATS. These tools don't talk to each other. This breaks data authenticity. There is no unified layer that checks candidate identity across every tool in your hiring process. Impersonators exploit this fragmentation — using proxies for assessments, stand-ins for interviews, and bait-and-switch on Day One — knowing the tools will never cross-reference each other.

Fragmented tools, no unified check

Your AI interview tool flags face-liveness. Your assessment platform detects remote desktop. Your ATS runs background checks. But none of these tools talk to each other. No single layer cross-references identity data across the entire flow — so a proxy candidate passes the assessment, a stand-in clears the interview, and a different person shows up on Day One.

Each tool sees one piece of the puzzle

An AI interview tool might catch a deepfake. An assessment platform might flag keystroke anomalies. But disconnected signals mean nothing when they're not correlated. Impersonators exploit this — knowing that a proxy in the assessment won't be matched against the video interview face or the Day One identity check.

No confidence score across the pipeline

Without a unified layer that aggregates and analyzes data from every hiring tool, you never get a single answer to the most important question: is this candidate who they claim to be? Recruiters are left juggling isolated reports from 4+ tools, trying to manually spot impersonation — and failing.
